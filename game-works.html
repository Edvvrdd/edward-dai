Lambyrinth

Horror Puzzle Game | Audio Lead & Technical Implementation

A horror puzzle game where a lonely shepherd must rescue her lost sheep from a dark, maze-like forest before a lurking monster finds them. I led the complete audio direction, creating an immersive soundscape that builds tension through responsive voice systems and terrifying audio moments.

Tools: FMOD, Reaper
Duration: 4 months

Technical Implementation

Responsive VO System

Built using FMOD parameters tracking player location, object proximity, and monster state. The system triggers contextual dialogue based on multiple game variables, creating a character that feels alive and connected to the player's actions.

  • Proximity-based dialogue triggers
  • Emotional state transitions based on threat level
  • Inactivity detection for ambient character moments

Mimic Monster Audio

Captures microphone input, processes it with distortion and reverb effects in real-time, and triggers it as a 3D sound event from the monster's location. This creates one of the game's most memorable and terrifying moments.

  • Real-time microphone input capture
  • Dynamic audio processing (distortion, pitch shift, reverb)
  • 3D spatial audio positioning based on monster location

Challenges & Solutions

Challenge: Creating a sense of dread in an open forest environment
Solution: Layered ambience with dynamic mixing based on player location, using silence and subtle sounds to build tension rather than constant noise
Challenge: Making the monster feel present without being visible
Solution: Implemented directional audio cues, breathing sounds, and the voice mimicry system to create psychological presence

Results & Learnings

Leading the audio direction for Lambyrinth taught me how to maintain a cohesive sonic vision while managing a wide range of assets. The voice mimicry mechanic became one of the most talked-about features, demonstrating the power of audio to create truly memorable gameplay moments. This project deepened my understanding of FMOD's parameter-driven systems and reinforced the importance of designing sound that shapes the player's emotional journey.
